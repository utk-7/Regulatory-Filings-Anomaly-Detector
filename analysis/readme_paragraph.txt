This accruals-based ratio set is deliberately scoped for non-financial companies, where operating cash flows and balance sheet accruals provide meaningful signals of earnings quality. Banks such as India's Yes Bank (which faced a crisis in 2020) require a fundamentally different approach—focusing on asset quality metrics like non-performing asset (NPA) ratios, provisioning coverage, and loan loss provisions—because their business model centers on debt intermediation rather than product sales. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI), not SEBI or the SEC, is the relevant regulator for Indian banks, with its own disclosure requirements and supervisory framework. This scoping is a purposeful starting point, and extending the system to banks would involve developing RBI-compliant NPA and provisioning ratios as a natural next extension, rather than a gap in the current design.
